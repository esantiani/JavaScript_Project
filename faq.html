FAQ

Do you place dogs outside of Southern California?
No, we do not place German Shepherds outside of Southern California. Due to our limited resources and the complexities involved in out-of-state placements, we focus solely on local adoptions. We encourage German Shepherd lovers in other areas to check their local shelters for dogs in need.
Where do your German Shepherds come from?
About 50% of our dogs come from owners who can no longer keep them, while the other 50% are rescued from animal shelters when they are at risk of euthanasia.
Are the rescue dogs trained?
Some of our German Shepherds come with basic training. However, we recommend adopters enroll their new companions in a basic obedience class to help reinforce positive behavior and create a strong bond.
Are all rescued dogs abused or sick?
No, this is a common misconception. While a few of our dogs may have experienced neglect or abuse, most are surrendered due to lifestyle changes. Many are happy, healthy, and simply in need of a second chance.
Can I adopt an unaltered (not spayed or neutered) dog?
No. All dogs adopted through GSROC are spayed or neutered before placement. This policy reflects our commitment to ending pet overpopulation and promoting responsible pet ownership.
